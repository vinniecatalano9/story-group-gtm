_Generated 2026-06-08 from a 17-agent analysis of every Instantly + HeyReach responder. Supersedes the v2 firmographic filter._

## Definition
**Story Group Final ICP — the sole-decider founder/operator of a real, named company who books on LinkedIn.**

The single durable buyer signature across every genuine booked or scheduled call in the 374-line responder set is narrow and behavioral, not demographic: a person who controls the budget for their own brand, runs a real named operating entity (not a personal brand or solo practice with no team), and demonstrates buying intent in their reply text — proposing or accepting a specific time, self-booking, asking for calendar logistics, or routing to their own coordinator. That is the whole game. Title fit and vertical fit are SOURCING HINTS only; they do not predict "yes." Proof: the cleanest title-and-vertical matches in the file said no — John MD/iPill ("No thanks"), Divan Dave/OmniMD (routed to VP), Payal MD ("only if free"), Alejandro Fernandez (health+AI, the "richest pocket," "No thanks"), Waseem/After Hours Urgent Care, Laura Miller/TempDev ("already engaged with a PR company"), Jay Schweid/ephelants ("we have a PR Partner"), and Mark Westlake/GearBrain whose exact title "CEO & Founder" was literally on the draft whitelist ("all set with our outreach"). Meanwhile most real bookers FAIL a literal title match: Ishmael Osekre (the file's only self-booker, "Founder & Principal Architect"), Saverio Pulizzi ("2x Founder"), Tarshann Washington ("Founder, Strix"), Shomir Dutt / Paul Dagum (lose on "and" vs "&"), Todd Sega ("Managing Director of Pharmacy at Innovaccer"), Nikita Chizhov ("Inventor"), Alex Alaniz ("Founding Engineer"), Joel Idelson ("CEO / Private Equity / Startup"), Joy MPH (no title token at all). So: read the headline SEMANTICALLY for ownership/sole-decider authority, score the REPLY TEXT for intent, and run a hard disqualify-override on objection language regardless of how perfect the profile looks.

**Channel:** LinkedIn-first is decisive — every confirmed booking except Tom Ho came via LinkedIn, and the entire email cohort was dominated by machine artifacts (OOO, departed-employee, verification walls) and one-word passes from finance/insurance/credit domains. But email is NOT zero and NOT finance-exclusively-dead: Marc Shenkman (a FINANCE CEO at priorityfinancial.net) negotiated meeting times across three emails, Nancy Lee/ablsoft ("happy to chat"), and Tom Ho booked by email. Treat email as a secondary, low-yield channel for verified/monitored inboxes with a warm reason — do not hard-block finance domains, but do de-prioritize the compliance-bound finance/insurance/credit cohort because as a SOURCE it converted near-zero.

**Verticals:** Healthcare / healthtech founder-CEOs are the densest converting pocket by booked-call volume (Chad Greene/PERIOME, Reza Moradi/Eyeconic, Carrie Allen/Holistic Health Hub, Todd Sega/Innovaccer, Nikita Chizhov/Rehabwheel, Derek Fullerton, LaToya Watkins). Tech/AI founders are #2 with the best buyer economics and natural press appetite (Tarshann/Strix, Christopher/TeroAI, Paul Dagum/Applied Cognition, Owen Fitzgerald/Ridge, Shomir Dutt/Intro, Saverio Pulizzi, Omer Jamal). The healthtech-AI overlap is the richest single sourcing pocket. BUT vertical is a coloring/sourcing input, never a kill gate on a warm responder — De'Shawn (faith/education) and Damon Araujo (education) both gave hot scheduling replies, so do not auto-kill education/nonprofit on sector label; kill on absence of P&L/budget signal in the message instead.

**Geography:** US core, EST/CST business hours — every genuine booking proposed an EST slot. De-prioritize travel-gated-abroad (Databento Asia OOO). One Africa OTT signal (Wi-flix) is a pleasantry, not a target — OTT is speculative, not a stated vertical.

**Net rule:** Source on LinkedIn for sole-decider founders/owners/operators of real named companies in health or tech/AI (US, EST/CST). Then ignore title/vertical at the responder stage and SCORE ON REPLY TEXT: proposes/accepts a time, self-books, asks for invite logistics, or routes to their own coordinator = HOT. Unconditional "tell me more" = WARM. And hard-kill on first sight of free/cost-to-me/pay-to-play/"invest money," "already have/engaged a PR firm," or a flat no — even on a textbook-perfect profile.
