# The LinkedIn Reply Playbook
### Cold outreach to CEOs and founders, via HeyReach — Story Group internal strategy doc

---

## 1. Thesis: what actually moves reply rate

Reply rate is won before you write a word. The three biggest levers — in order — are **who you target, whether you warm the prospect before connecting, and whether your first post-accept message is about THEM with a low-effort question instead of a pitch.** Copy matters, but for a CEO/founder audience it is co-equal with targeting, not subordinate: the single cleanest controlled variable in the research swung CEO reply rate **2.45x** purely on hook choice (timeline hook 10.44% vs problem hook 4.26%, cold email — TheDigitalBloom, https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/cold-outbound-reply-rate-benchmarks/). So you fix the list AND the hook; neither alone gets you there. Everything else (notes, voice notes, video, send-time) is a tuning knob worth single-digit points at most.

### Ranked levers (highest leverage first)

1. **Targeting / list warmth.** Warm and intent-based lists beat cold lists ~2-4x on reply (triangulated: Belkins multi-touch 2.43x; LinkedSDR signal-based 3-4x). Below ~8% reply, it's almost always the list, not the copy (LinkedSDR, https://linkedsdr.com/blog/linkedin-outreach-benchmarks-in-2026).
2. **Warm-up before connecting (multi-action).** Message + profile visit replies at **11.87%** vs **4.88%** message-only and **3.53%** request-only — ~2.4-3.4x, the strongest single measured lever in the 20M-attempt dataset (Belkins/Expandi, https://belkins.io/blog/linkedin-outreach-study).
3. **The post-accept first message.** Platform message reply holds at **~10.4%** while connection-note reply has fallen to **2.2%** and is dropping. Win the reply in the thread, not the note (Expandi 2026, https://expandi.io/blog/linkedin-outreach-benchmarks-2026/).
4. **A specific, them-focused relevance hook + one soft CTA.** Two custom personalization attributes lifted cold-email reply to 5.6% vs 3.6% (+56%, Hunter 31M emails, https://hunter.io/the-state-of-cold-email/). Soft/interest CTAs beat hard meeting asks ~3x in cold email (4.2% vs 1.4%, Puzzle Inbox, https://puzzleinbox.com/blog/cold-email-cta-best-practices/).
5. **Follow-up #2 (not #1).** On LinkedIn the 1st follow-up gets ~0.6% *fewer* replies than none; the 2nd gets **+4.05%**. Put the real ask on touch 2 (Expandi/Belkins 20M).
6. **Sender profile quality.** A silent gate — prospects check it before replying. Photo + complete profile + problem-led headline. Cheap to fix, but a ceiling, not a multiplier.
7. **Connection note, voice, video, send-time.** Marginal. Worth testing, not worth building the strategy on.

**The one number to internalize:** plan your cold CEO/founder campaigns against **~28.5% acceptance and ~10.4% message reply** (Expandi, 13.2M requests). Treat 15-25% reply as "strong, well-targeted," and 30%+ as a rare top-decile outcome — not a target you staff against.

## 2. The sequence (copy-pasteable)

Tuned for cold outreach to busy executives. Connection + 3 messages, max 4 touches, ending in a break-up. **7-day gaps**, not 4 — both HeyReach and Phantombuster build their winning cadences on ~7-day spacing, and it keeps you safely inside account limits.

### Cadence at a glance

| Touch | Day | Action | Job |
|------|-----|--------|-----|
| Pre-touch | Day −2 | View profile + like/comment one recent post | Warm-up (the 11.87% lever) |
| T0 | Day 0 | Connection request — **no note** | Get connected |
| T1 | Day 0-1 after accept | First message — relevance hook + soft question | Earn a reply, no pitch |
| T2 | Day +7 | Follow-up — the real (still soft) ask | This is where the lift lives |
| T3 | Day +14 | Break-up message | Pressure-removal, free reply |

> **Why no note:** a note does not raise acceptance (26.42% with vs 26.37% without, 20M study) and note-reply is collapsing (3.5%→2.2% YoY). The reply lever is the post-accept message, which holds at ~10.4%. Skip the note, spend the personalization budget on T1. *(If you want to A/B a note, keep it ≤300 chars and purely relevance, no ask — but the no-note arm is the default.)*

---

### T0 — Connection request
**No note.** (If your warm-up touch at Day −2 was a comment on their post, the request now lands as "the person who engaged with me," which is the entire point.)

---

### T1 — First message (Day 0-1 after accept)
The only job is to earn a reply. No pitch. One specific them-hook + one low-friction question. 50-90 words, ~3:1 them:us.

**Variation A — recent content / POV hook**
```
Hey [First] — your post on [specific topic / the take on X] stuck with me,
especially the bit about [specific detail]. Most [their-title]s I talk to
are sitting on a stronger story than they're getting credit for publicly.

Curious — is getting [company] (or you) more visible in [their world / trade press]
something that's actually on your radar this year, or not really a priority right now?
```

**Variation B — trigger / milestone hook** (funding, expansion, leadership change, new product)
```
Hey [First] — saw [company] [specific trigger: closed the round / opened the
[city] office / launched X]. Congrats. Usually that's the exact moment a
founder's profile should be climbing and it… just doesn't, on its own.

Are you doing anything deliberate on the visibility/press side right now,
or is it still on the "later" pile?
```

**Variation C — no clean signal (pure relevance)**
```
Hey [First] — connected because [company] keeps coming up in the [industry] world
and I work with a lot of [their-title]s on the same thing: they're respected by the
people who already know them, and invisible to everyone who doesn't.

Is closing that gap something you're thinking about this year, or are you heads-down
on other stuff?
```

**Notes:** No link, no calendar, no "quick call." The question is binary and easy to answer "not right now" — which still opens the thread. Avoid obvious AI tells; 69% of decision-makers are bothered by AI-sounding outreach and 65% say cold messages feel too sales-focused (Hunter 31M, https://hunter.io/the-state-of-cold-email/).

---

### T2 — Follow-up (Day +7) — **the real ask lives here**
This is the highest-value touch (+4.05% vs the −0.6% from touch 1). Still soft, but now you can show one proof point and make a concrete, low-effort offer.

**Variation A — value-forward**
```
[First] — no worries if the timing's off. One thing that might be useful regardless:
we recently got [comparable client/industry] [specific outcome — e.g. into [Outlet]
and two trade podcasts in ~60 days].

If it'd help, I can send a quick 2-min breakdown of how we'd approach it for [company] —
no call needed. Want me to drop it here?
```

**Variation B — single-question re-engage**
```
[First] — circling back once. If you were going to raise [company]'s public profile
this year, what's the thing actually stopping it — bandwidth, not sure it's worth it,
or just not the priority right now?
```

**Why "send you something here, no call needed":** it's the soft/interest CTA the data rewards, and it keeps the meeting ask out until the prospect is warm. Gong's 304K-email study shows specific time-asks only start winning at the *deal* stage, not cold (https://www.gong.io/blog/this-surprising-cold-email-cta-will-help-you-book-a-lot-more-meetings).

---

### T3 — Break-up (Day +14)
Removes pressure, reliably shakes loose a chunk of non-responders (practitioner consensus ~15-25% of remaining; directional, not an A/B-measured number). Keep it warm and genuinely final.

```
[First] — I'll leave it here so I'm not cluttering your inbox. If raising
[company]'s profile ever moves up the list, I'm easy to find. Either way,
rooting for what you're building.
```

*(Optional softer last-line variant: "…and if the answer's just 'not now,' a one-word reply tells me to check back in Q[X] instead of bugging you.")*

**Stop rules:** stop the sequence the instant they reply (route to inbox workflow). Stop after T3 regardless. Never add a 4th cold follow-up — email data shows the 4th touch triples spam/unsubscribe and reply craters (Belkins 16.5M).

### The pipeline math you should actually plan against
For a **harder-than-average CEO/founder cold audience**, plan conservatively:
- **Acceptance:** 22-30% (founders are hard to *reach* — acceptance drops with seniority).
- **Reply (of accepted):** ~10%, pushing to 15-20% with strong targeting + warm-up.
- **Positive reply:** ~3-6% of sends is a planning heuristic (🔴 not measured); plan toward the **low end** for this ICP.
- **Booked meetings:** ~1-3% of sends, **plan toward ~1%** until your own data says otherwise. HeyReach's own band: 20-30% of *positive* replies convert to meetings.

**Where the data is thin / don't quote these:** the 38% vs 4% "signal-timed" gap (🔴 refuted — real lift is ~2-3x), the 340% / 58% personalization lifts (🔴 no source), the 287% multichannel stat (🔴 ecommerce purchase data, not outbound), the "16x weekend acceptance drop" (🔴 one un-sized agency dataset), and any 30-50% reply "benchmark" (🔴 hand-optimized outliers). The 39-42% HeyReach case studies are real but warm/tiny-sample ceilings — never plan against them.

## 4. HeyReach setup

### Sender rotation (this is the whole reason to use HeyReach)
Scale comes from **more senders, not more activity per seat.** LinkedIn caps connection requests at ~100/week per account regardless of plan — Sales Navigator does **not** raise it. Assign N accounts to one campaign; HeyReach auto-spreads invites so each stays under its own daily/weekly cap.
- ~5 senders on one campaign = ~100-125 invites/day total while each account stays in the safe band.
- HeyReach gives each account a **dedicated static residential IP that is never shared** — keep it that way; never reuse an IP across accounts.
- HeyReach freezes an account near ~100 invites/week (cannot override) and hard-caps **200 total actions/day** (views + likes + messages + invites — *not* 200 invites).

### Safe limits per account
| Account state | Connection requests/day | Weekly | Total actions/day |
|---|---|---|---|
| New (post-warmup, first outreach weeks) | ramp 10→20 | 25→100 over 4 weeks | start ~40 |
| Established | **15-20/day** (target) | **<100** (aim ~80 for buffer) | up to 200 (cap) |

The **weekly cap binds before the daily one** (20/day × 5 = 100). Manage acceptance rate, not just counts — the real restriction trigger is low acceptance + cold/3rd-degree + "I don't know this person" flags, not a single daily number.

### Sequence structure in-app
Build it as: **View Profile → (manual or separate) like → Connection Request → Delay 7d → Message → Delay 7d → Message → Delay 7d → Message (break-up).** Note HeyReach's automated steps are *only*: connection request, message, InMail, view profile, follow, like post. **Voice notes are NOT an automated step** — manual-from-desktop-inbox only (confirm current status with HeyReach support before planning any voice play).

### A/B testing
HeyReach has native A/B testing. Test **one variable at a time**, in this priority order:
1. **List/segment** (biggest lever) — same copy, two audiences.
2. **T1 hook** (content vs trigger vs pure-relevance).
3. **T2 CTA** (value-drop vs single-question).
- Run two variants max; aim for ~100 leads/variant before calling it (directional rule of thumb, validate in-app). Judge on **positive reply rate**, not raw reply rate — it's the truer ICP-fit signal.

### Inbox workflow
- **Reply = stop sequence instantly** and route to a human (you). HeyReach's positive-reply auto-detect can triage warm ones, but read every C-suite reply yourself.
- Check the unified inbox **2x/day**, respond inside business hours. 21% of acceptances happen within 60 min and 63% within 24h — the thread is hottest immediately.
- This is where your manual voice-note play lives (see §7), if you run one — priority accounts only.

### List import
- Import pre-filtered. Run your Apollo/Sales-Nav/HeyReach export through the **ICP lead-filter first** (drop solo operators / non-decision-makers) before it hits any campaign. A clean small list out-replies a big dirty one every time.
- Segment lists by trigger/warmth so you can A/B audience cleanly and fire warm signals inside the 24-hour window.

## 5. Targeting beats copy (list building)

This is the highest-leverage section in the doc. **Below ~8% reply, fix the list before touching copy.** For CEOs/founders, audit copy in parallel — but list quality is what moves you from a 5% campaign to a 15% one.

### The hierarchy of list warmth (best → worst)
1. **Engagement / intent signals** (warmest): people who viewed your profile, engaged with your or a competitor's post, attended a relevant event, commented on an industry thread. Fire within the **24-hour window** — these decay fast. Defensible lift: **2-4x** over cold (not the 18-31x vendor marketing).
2. **Trigger-based cold:** funding, leadership change, expansion, new product, hiring comms, recent press (or conspicuous *absence* of press). This is your bread and butter for PR — the trigger is also your T1 hook.
3. **ICP-cold (Sales Nav):** tightly filtered but no signal. Acceptable at volume; expect platform-average rates.
4. **Spray-and-pray:** 2-5% acceptance. Don't.

### Sales Navigator filters that matter for PR-to-CEO
- **Seniority:** Owner / Founder / CXO / Partner / VP.
- **Company headcount:** filter on **employee count, not revenue** (Apollo/Sales Nav are reliable on headcount, noisy on revenue). Set a floor that screens out solo operators — the #1 source of unqualified booked calls.
- **Geography + industry:** lean toward verticals that actually accept. VC/PE-adjacent founders accept **34.9%** and reply **11.0%** — above the platform mean and a natural PR fit (Expandi). Avoid building a whole campaign in low-acceptance consumer verticals.
- **Posted-on-LinkedIn-recently** filter → instant T1 content hook (someone with a recent post is a warmer, more reachable target).
- **Changed jobs in last 90 days** → leadership-change trigger, one of the strongest PR hooks.
- **Exclude** anyone where company name ≈ person's name, "Self-Employed," "Independent," "Freelance," or headline matches "I help…/1:1 coaching/solo practitioner." (Your lead-filter already does this — enforce it on every list.)

### Honest C-suite reframe
C-suite is **hard to reach, not hard to convert.** Acceptance falls sharply with seniority (VP+ ~22% vs ICs ~41%), but once connected, C-level reply at **6.98%** — mid-pack, *above* Sales (6.32%) and Marketing (6.40%), below HR (12.08%). So: budget for lower acceptance to founders, lean on warm-up to lift it, and don't believe anyone who tells you C-suite is a dead audience on reply. The "+23% C-suite premium" floating around is cold-*email* data and doesn't transfer to LinkedIn.

### Quality-vs-quantity, stated honestly
A 150-person warm list at 32% acceptance yields 48 connections; a 500-person cold list at 12% yields 60. The big cold list "wins" on raw connections — but the warm list wins decisively on **replies, positive replies, and meeting quality**, which is the only scoreboard that matters. **Measure positive replies, not connection count.**

## 6. Account safety (don't torch the fleet)

The 2026 environment is hostile: LinkedIn banned HeyReach's founder and removed its company page in March 2026, and is enforcing at the **cloud-proxy infrastructure level**, not just per-account. That's the real durable risk — mitigate by staying conservative, keeping acceptance high, and not betting the whole agency on one tool's proxy layer. (Ignore the unsourced "~40% of cloud-tool accounts restricted in Q1 2026" claim — it's not in the cited source.)

### Hard limits (plan to these)
- **~100 connection invites/week per account — for ALL account types.** Sales Nav/Premium does not raise it.
- **15-20 invites/day** on established accounts (weekly cap binds first).
- **200 total actions/day** hard ceiling (HeyReach auto-pauses).
- **Dedicated static residential IP per account, never shared.**

### Warmup for a new sender (the part everyone skips and regrets)
1. **Weeks 1-4 (passive, NO connection requests):** profile views, likes, light comments, post occasionally. Build the account so the profile looks real before it does anything.
2. **Week 4:** quarter volume — ~25-35 invites/week.
3. **Week 5:** ~50/week.
4. **Week 6:** ~75/week.
5. **Week 7:** full ~100/week.

Firing 50+ invites from a cold account in week one very likely gets it restricted within 1-2 weeks (vendor consensus; the exact ">70%" is an estimate, not a study). With a fleet, a botched warmup doesn't cost one account — it patterns across the IP range.

### Acceptance is your safety gauge, not just a KPI
- **Above 30%:** healthy, scale.
- **Under ~20% sustained:** LinkedIn throttles your weekly allowance to as low as ~50/week. **Pause and fix targeting/profile before pushing volume.**
- Low acceptance + "I don't know this person" reports = the fast path to restriction.

### Operational
- Schedule sends around HeyReach's **Monday 2am PT reset** — front-load the week so freezes don't strand accounts mid-week.
- Don't run identical copy across the whole fleet simultaneously; vary it (spintax-style) so the pattern isn't trivially detectable.

## 7. Sender profile, voice/video, timing, multichannel

### Sender profile — the silent conversion gate
Prospects check your profile before they accept or reply. Fix these **before** sending a single invite. The strongest verified fact here: **sender seniority barely matters** (C-level senders 29.4% vs junior 26.3% acceptance — a 3-point spread), so for a multi-account agency, invest in *profile quality*, not in buying or aging "senior-looking" accounts.

Checklist:
- **Professional photo.** Highest-leverage static fix. (LinkedIn claims "up to 21x more views" — old, "up to," directional — but the direction is real.)
- **Problem-led headline:** audience + value, not job title. (e.g. "Helping founders get the press their company already deserves" beats "VP of Growth, Story Group.")
- **Complete profile:** banner, about section, 5+ skills, recent activity. LinkedIn's own data: complete profiles see ~+87% InMail acceptance.
- **Recent, credible posting cadence** — so a prospect who clicks through sees a real operator, not a shell.

### Voice notes — **manual-only, priority accounts only.** Verdict: marginal, test it, don't build on it.
- HeyReach **cannot automate** voice notes. They're manual from the desktop inbox; LinkedIn voice notes cap at **60s** (script to 30-45s) and are natively mobile-only.
- The "+30-40% / 2x lift" figures are all vendor marketing (La Growth Machine sells the feature). One independent source saw ~30%. **Treat 2x as a ceiling, not an expectation.**
- **Use case:** a manual voice note as your T2 touch on a small priority segment (warm signals, dream accounts). Not a fleet-wide automated play — the labor cost doesn't pencil out at scale, and the AI-voice-clone API route carries ban/authenticity risk.

### Video — better evidence than voice, same "sequence it, don't lead with it" rule. Verdict: worth a priority-segment test.
- Plan to **3-5x text reply** on well-targeted lists (16% typical personalized vs 1-5% text); 22-30% are top-decile outliers, not the expectation for cold execs.
- **Never attach video** — link a thumbnail/GIF to a hosted page (animated thumbnails get ~2-3x the clicks of a static play button).
- Keep it 60-90s, prospect name + company in-frame (that personalization is what separates 16% from a marginal generic-video lift). Best real proof point to cite internally: Intercom's documented +19% reply from Loom (skip the unverifiable Chili Piper and stale 2017 Terminus +216% numbers).

### Timing — set it and stop fiddling
- **Send Tue-Thu (Mon fine), 9-11am in the recipient's local time.** Segment lists by region so "9-11am" means their morning, not yours.
- **Day-of-week barely moves replies** — the full spread across 20M attempts is 6.40%→6.90% (noise). Don't over-engineer it. It moves *acceptance* a bit more, but trust the direction (weekday >> weekend), not the inflated "16x" multiple.
- Strike fast on acceptance: 63% of accepts happen within 24h, 21% within the first hour. T1 should fire same-day or next-day.

### LinkedIn + email multichannel — coordinated, not parallel. Verdict: worth it, sequenced.
- **Lead with LinkedIn** (~10.4% message reply) and use email as a **coordinated second channel** (~3.4-4% single-channel), not a simultaneous blast.
- Multichannel lift is real but cite it **conservatively at ~3-3.5x** (La Growth Machine vendor number) — and never the 287% ecommerce stat.
- For CEOs specifically, email is where the seniority premium actually shows up (C-level reply ~3.3x more than managers at a 14.16% *positive* reply rate on email, Sales.co/Prospeo). Practical move: if a founder accepts but goes quiet after T1, a single well-timed email referencing the LinkedIn thread is a legitimate touch — not a separate cold sequence.

## 8. What changed in 2026 / what's dying

- **AI saturation is the dominant force.** ~Half of inbox spam is now AI-generated (Barracuda); **69% of decision-makers are bothered by obvious AI**, 65% say cold messages feel too sales-focused, 61% ignore for lack of relevance, 48% flag generic copy (Hunter 31M). The tell-tale AI-generated DM is now a *liability*, not a time-saver.
- **Use AI for the angle, a human for the sentence.** This is measured, not opinion: AI-*assisted* first messages reply **4.19% vs 2.60%** (+61%), but AI-*written* follow-ups *underperform* (3.48% vs 3.91%). Let AI find the trigger; write the message yourself.
- **Relevance over volume is now structural, not just advice.** The ~100-invite/week cap and seniority-blind acceptance mean you literally cannot out-volume the problem — you have to out-target it.
- **The connection note is dying as a lever.** Note-reply fell 37% in a year (3.5%→2.2%). Stop optimizing it; move the work to the post-accept message.
- **Cold channels are decaying broadly.** Cold email reply: 8.5% (2019) → 5% (2025) → ~3.4-4% (2026). LinkedIn message reply has held at 10-11% — which is exactly why it's your lead channel — but assume the trend pressure is downward and that targeting precision is your only durable edge.
- **Platform/vendor risk is real.** LinkedIn is actively enforcing against automation infrastructure (the HeyReach founder ban). Don't single-thread the agency's entire pipeline through one tool's proxy layer.

## 9. Do this Monday

1. **Fix every sender profile first** — photo, problem-led headline, complete about/skills, recent post. No sending until this is done. (1 hr/account.)
2. **Re-run your current list through the ICP lead-filter** — drop solo operators and non-decision-makers. A clean list is the single biggest reply-rate move available today.
3. **Build two segmented lists in Sales Nav:** (a) "posted recently" CEOs/founders for content-hook T1, (b) "changed jobs in 90 days / recent funding" for trigger-hook T1. Tag by warmth.
4. **Turn on the Day −2 warm-up touch** (view profile + like/comment) in the sequence — this is the 11.87% lever and most people skip it.
5. **Load the connection-no-note + 3-message sequence** (T1/T2/T3 at Day 0/+7/+14) with Variations A/B/C swapped by list type.
6. **Set safe limits:** 15-20 invites/day/account, <100/week, warmup any account under ~4 weeks old. Confirm each account has its own dedicated IP.
7. **Schedule sends Tue-Thu 9-11am recipient-local**, front-loaded toward the Monday HeyReach reset.
8. **Start one A/B:** same copy, two audiences (warm-signal list vs ICP-cold) — prove the targeting lever to yourself with your own numbers.
9. **Set the inbox habit:** check 2x/day, reply same business day, stop-sequence-on-reply on.

## 10. Metrics to track (with thresholds)

Track these five weekly, per campaign and per sender account. Judge on **positive reply rate** above all — it's the truest ICP-fit signal.

| Metric | Floor (fix it) | Healthy | Strong |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Connection acceptance** | <20% → pause, fix targeting/profile | 25-35% | 40%+ |
| **Message reply rate** (of accepted) | <8% → list problem, not copy | 10-15% | 20%+ |
| **Positive reply rate** | <20% of replies | 30%+ | 40%+ |
| **Positive reply → meeting** | <20% | 20-30% | 30%+ |
| **Booked meetings (of sends)** | — | ~1% (plan here for CEOs) | 2-3% |

**Account-health guardrails (check before scaling any account):** acceptance ≥30% = scale; 20-30% = hold and tune; <20% sustained = stop and fix before LinkedIn throttles you to ~50/week. Keep invites <100/week/account and total actions <200/day. If reply quality drops while volume holds, it's the list — re-segment before you re-write.
