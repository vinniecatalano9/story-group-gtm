# Story Group — ICP & Outreach Playbook
*PR & earned-media for business executives. Primary offer: earned media/PR ($4K–$22K/mo). Owner: Vincent Catalano, VP of Growth. Last updated June 2026.*

---

## 0. The Core Principle: Score on Behavior, Source on Firmographics

**The buyer signature is BEHAVIORAL, not demographic.** Stop trying to predict who buys from their title or vertical. Two CEOs of identical health-tech startups will give opposite replies — one books a call, one replies "Is this free?" The difference isn't on their LinkedIn profile. It's in how they respond.

Two jobs, two different inputs. Don't mix them:

| Job | What you use | What it answers |
|---|---|---|
| **SOURCING** (who to email) | Vertical + firmographics (industry, headcount, funding, title, geo) | "Is this person *plausibly* a fit?" |
| **SCORING** (who to chase) | The reply itself — intent, money-readiness, urgency | "Is this person *actually* going to buy?" |

**The single highest-signal behavior in our entire dataset: a cost question *before* a no.** When a prospect asks "What's the cost?" / "Is there a fee?" / "What's the investment?" *before* rejecting you, they have already accepted the premise (they want press) and are now pricing it. That is a buying motion. Compare:

- **BUY MOTION** — Stephanie NP-BC: *"Is there a cost to me in this? What types of folks are you working with…"* (asking price, engaged, wants detail → book it)
- **DEAD** — Dr. Isler (Richmond County Schools HR): *"Is this free?"* (asking price as a *gate to qualify out* — a free-or-nothing buyer → kill)
- **DEAD** — Payal M.D.: *"I would only be interested if the media attention is free of cost. Otherwise I will pass."* (the free-or-nothing tell, explicitly → kill)

Same surface question ("cost?"), opposite intent. **Read the motion, not the keyword.** A genuine cost question with curiosity attached = your best lead. A cost question that's really a "prove it's free" test = your worst.

**Why this matters for the team:** our funnel leaks at sourcing (too many plausible-but-dead verticals) *and* wastes rep time on warm-but-broke replies that never convert. This playbook fixes both: tighter sourcing (converting verticals + anti-ICP) and disciplined scoring (the reply guide at the end).

## 1. The Converting Verticals

Four verticals reliably convert. For each: why they buy, triggers to time outreach, who signs, the angle, outlets, objections, and exactly how to source them.

A constant across all four — **the #1 objection everywhere is "Is this pay-to-play?"** Memorize the reframe: *We don't buy placements. We earn them — reporters choose to write because it's news. That editorial independence is the entire reason the coverage moves money and decisions. The moment it's paid, your buyers discount it.* We saw this kill a deal in the wild: Perry Grosser — *"this is a pay to play game and the fees… are out of line"* — and Ziad Abdelnour flipped the instant money was implied (*"I am not paying anything to be covered"*). Lead with editorial independence or lose them.

### 1A. Health / Biotech / Pharma Founder-CEOs
*Therapeutics, diagnostics, medtech, digital/clinical health — seed through Series C / pre-IPO.*

**Why they buy PR**
- **Fundraising leverage** — a STAT/Endpoints/Fierce Biotech story is read by the exact VCs writing the next check (RA Capital, Forbion, Foresite, Sanofi Ventures). Coverage de-risks the founder before the pitch meeting. Rounds are milestone-gated, so every raise forces a visibility push.
- **Credibility / category creation** — novel-modality founders need third-party proof the science is real, not vaporware.
- **Recruiting** — top PhDs, a CSO, a clinical lead join companies they've heard of. Visibility shortens the cycle and wins hires against better-funded incumbents.
- **Partner & regulatory trust** — pharma BD/licensing/M&A moves faster when the acquirer already knows the name.
- **KOL / patient / physician trust** and **founder legacy** as the authority on the disease area.

**Trigger events** (highest-intent first)
1. **New round closed / extension / crossover** — *the* top trigger; fresh budget + a real announcement need.
2. Positive clinical readout (Ph1 safety, Ph2 efficacy, topline Ph3) or upcoming data catalyst at a congress.
3. FDA milestone: IND clearance, Fast Track / Breakthrough / Orphan Drug, PDUFA date, AdCom, approval.
4. Product/platform launch, first-in-human dosing, major publication (Nature/Cell/NEJM/Science).
5. Pharma partnership / licensing / co-development signed (esp. named big-pharma partner).
6. Marquee exec hire (CSO, CMO, CCO) or board/SAB addition.
7. M&A activity or starting the IPO/S-1 process.
8. Run-up to **JPM Healthcare (January)** or a major congress (ASCO, ASH, AHA).
9. Negative press / short-seller report (reactive — narrative control, fast).
10. New facility, manufacturing scale-up, first commercial revenue / first patients treated.

**Decision-maker** — Founder/CEO signs (almost always a scientist- or physician-founder), hands-on with the narrative because the science is their life's work. They defer on timing/budget to: **(1) VCs/board** — *"our investor wants more visibility before the next round"* is the strongest internal sponsor; board pressure accelerates the buy. **(2) Head of Comms / Chief of Staff / VP Marketing** if one exists (the gatekeeper at Series B+). On regulated messaging, also Regulatory/Legal and the CMO. *Pre-seed/seed → sell the CEO direct. Series B+ → expect a comms gatekeeper.*

**Messaging angle** — *"Your science is real, but the people who fund, partner with, and join you only believe what STAT, Endpoints, and Fierce tell them. We make sure your story is told there before your next raise — not after. Earned coverage is the third-party proof that turns a complex platform into a fundable, partner-grade, must-hire company."*

**Proof points** — features/quotes in STAT, Endpoints, Fierce Biotech, BioPharma Dive, BioSpace; funding coverage in the outlets investors read; founder bylines/op-eds; inclusion in "companies to watch" / funding trackers ahead of JPM; podcast/panel placements (STAT's Readout LOUD, JPM/BIO/HLTH stages); downstream outcomes in their language — warmer investor meetings, BD inbound, easier recruiting.

**Target outlets** — **STAT News** (Adam Feuerstein, Damian Garde, Matthew Herper, Meghana Keshavan), **Endpoints News** (funding + milestones), **Fierce Biotech** (Fierce Fundraising Tracker), **BioPharma Dive**, **BioSpace**; crossover: Forbes (Healthcare/30u30), Bloomberg, Reuters Health, Axios Pro Health Tech, Fortune Brainstorm Health; digital-health/medtech: Fierce Healthcare, MedCity News, MM+M; prestige anchor: Nature Biotechnology, Science, Cell/NEJM.

**Objections → reframes**
- *Pay-to-play?* → In biotech the distinction is everything — investors and pharma BD **discount sponsored content** and trust earned coverage precisely because a reporter chose to write it. You pay for our access, judgment, and the work — never the placement.
- *Quiet period / embargo / pre-IPO* → We work inside Reg FD, quiet periods, and embargoes *with* your legal/IR team. We build positioning and reporter relationships now so the moment data or the S-1 clears, you launch from strength.
- *My VCs / board handle comms* → Platform teams cover 40 companies and move on after the round. We're dedicated to your whole milestone cycle and make the board's job easier. Many of our founders were referred *by* their investors.
- *Too early / pre-clinical* → Early is the cheapest time to build the relationships and founder story that pay off at Series A and first readout. Reporters write about people and theses before they write about Ph3 data.
- *Guarantee STAT/Endpoints?* → No credible firm guarantees a logo — anyone who does is selling paid content. We guarantee strategy, relationships, pitch quality, and qualified shots on goal. These are the outlets we'd pursue first.
- *Tried PR, got generic releases* → That's the wire-and-pray model. We do research-driven, milestone-timed pitching to named reporters on your exact modality.

**Where to source** — Crunchbase / PitchBook / Fierce Fundraising Tracker / Labiotech (seed–Series C, last 30–90 days); FDA & catalyst calendars (BiopharmaWatch, FDA Tracker, BPIQ, Benzinga FDA) for PDUFA/AdCom/Ph2-3 readouts; conference rosters (JPM, BIO, HLTH, ViVE, ASCO/ASH, Biotech Showcase); accelerators (YC bio, IndieBio, Petri, Nucleate, Flagship, JLABS); active-fund portfolio pages (RA Capital, Forbion, Foresite, ARCH, Third Rock, a16z Bio+Health, GV); LinkedIn raise/hire/readout posts; trade-press bylines themselves; SEC EDGAR S-1/Form D; **client + investor referrals (warmest path)**.

**Apollo / Sales Nav filters**
- Industries: Biotechnology; Pharmaceuticals; Medical Devices; Hospital & Health Care; Research (life sciences).
- Titles: CEO, Founder, Co-Founder, President · secondary: CSO, CMO, Head of Communications, VP Marketing, Chief of Staff.
- Headcount: **11–50 and 51–200** core; 1–10 for seed founder-direct; **cap ~500** (above = in-house pharma comms).
- Keywords: therapeutics, clinical-stage, gene/cell therapy, oncology, rare disease, precision medicine, drug discovery, diagnostics, synthetic biology, RNA, antibody, AI drug discovery, medtech.
- Signals: raised funding last 90 days (Seed/A/B/C); hiring R&D/clinical/commercial; HQ in Boston/Cambridge, SF Bay, San Diego, NYC, RTP, Seattle.
- **Exclude:** 1000+ employees (big pharma); CROs, generic distributors, staffing/consulting; solo "consultant" titles.

### 1B. Medtech / Digital Health / Health Services
*Two sub-segments we actually convert — tailor the pitch:*
- **(1) VC-backed health tech** (EMR, SaaS, platforms — Praxis EMR, Rehabwheel) → buys PR for fundraising / category creation.
- **(2) SMB / owner-operated health SERVICES** (home health, PT/rehab, advocacy — Body Helix, Royal Oaks Home Health, Medical Insurance Advocacy) → buys PR for patient trust, referral-source credibility, recruiting.

The buyer is the founder/CEO/owner who signs the check in both cases.

**Why they buy PR**
- **Patient & referral-source trust** — the buyer (patient, caregiver, referring physician, discharge planner) is choosing who to trust with a body. A Becker's / MedCity / local-news feature is third-party proof that converts where an ad can't.
- **Fundraising leverage (tech side)** — 2026 digital-health $ is concentrating in fewer, bigger AI deals; earned media is how a non-megadeal startup signals momentum and earns warm intros.
- **Category creation** — "the EMR built BY a clinician," "patient-side billing advocacy" — own a POV an incumbent (Epic, WebPT, the insurer) can't.
- **Recruiting clinicians/caregivers** — with caregiver shortage the #1 agency pain, being the in-the-news employer is a hiring weapon.
- **Regulatory/safety credibility** (HIPAA, CMS, FDA, accreditation, star ratings) and **exit optioning** — a media footprint raises the multiple in a roll-up market.

**Trigger events** — funding closed/raising (tech); FDA 510(k) / clinical publication (device); product/AI-feature launch; health-system or payer contract win; Inc. 5000 / Becker's "Great Leaders" / business-journal award (services love these); clinical/exec hire; expansion (new market, location, acquisition); accreditation milestone (Joint Commission, CHAP/ACHC, HITRUST, star-rating jump); reputation threat (review wave, billing dispute, regulatory inquiry — fast trigger); commentable industry inflection (caregiver shortage, Medicare cuts, AI-in-care).

**Decision-maker** — **Services owners:** the founder IS the decision-maker, often the only approver (may defer to spouse/co-owner or fractional marketer; watch for a gatekeeping office manager/EA — wedge is direct-to-founder). **Tech founders:** CEO decides, defers technically to Head of Marketing/Growth, needs board/investor optics for spend → frame PR as fundraising/sales infrastructure, not cost. Clinician-founders decide **emotionally on credibility, not impressions — sell the proof, not the reach.**

**Messaging angle** — *In healthcare, patients, referrers, investors, and acquirers all ask the same silent question first: "can I trust them?" — and an ad can't answer it. We get you cited as the trusted authority in the outlets your buyers and your next investor already read.* Services: *"We make you the home-health/rehab name families and discharge planners recognize."* Tech: *"We turn your milestones into the third-party proof that closes enterprise deals and warms your next round."*

**Proof points** — sub-segment-matched features (home-health founder in McKnight's / Home Health Care News; EMR founder in MedCity / Fierce Healthcare; device founder in MedTech Dive / STAT); bylined thought-leadership (Forbes Councils, Becker's column); "Great Leaders" / Inc. 5000 / "40 under 40" lists for the site + recruiting; expert-source quotes on breaking health stories; coverage tied to a number (referral volume, demo requests, recruiting applications, investor inbound); local/regional broadcast & business-journal hits for services owners.

**Target outlets** — **MedCity News** (Katie Adams; EIC Arundhati Parmar — EMR/SaaS), **Fierce Healthcare/Healthtech** (Heather Landi), **STAT** (Ed Silverman/Pharmalot — device/clinical), **MedTech Dive** (device + Rock Health funding), **Healthcare Dive**, **Modern Healthcare** (establishment credibility), **Becker's Hospital Review / Health IT** ("Great Leaders," guest columns), **Home Health Care News & McKnight's Home Care** (Liza Berger) / **McKnight's Senior Living** (Lois Bowers — *the* outlets for the Royal Oaks-type owner), Forbes/Forbes Councils, regional business journals (Atlanta Business Chronicle, ACBJ network) + local broadcast + Inc.

**Objections → reframes**
- *Pay-to-play / ads dressed as articles?* → Earned media is the opposite. A patient/referrer/investor trusts a Becker's or MedCity story *because* you couldn't pay for it. The moment it's paid, they discount it.
- *We're regulated — HIPAA/CMS, nervous about saying the wrong thing* → That caution is why you want a health-media partner. We never expose PHI or make clinical claims; we position you on the industry conversation and run messaging by you first. Done right, coverage *reinforces* your compliance posture.
- *Guarantee STAT/Forbes/Modern Healthcare?* → No legit firm can — and if they do, that's the pay-to-play you were right to fear. We guarantee a defined number of targeted outlets/journalists and real pitches; these are the outlets we'd pursue first.
- *I'm local — patients come from referrals and Google, not Forbes* → Agreed — we go where YOUR buyers look: McKnight's/Home Health Care News that referrers read, the regional business journal and local broadcast families see, and the awards you put on your site to recruit. National is a bonus.
- *Expensive, can't measure ROI like ads* → We tie it to numbers you already track — referral inbound, demos, recruiting applications, investor/acquirer interest. One home-health contract or one EMR deal is six figures; a stronger exec brand raises your exit multiple. Pipeline and exit insurance, not a line item.
- *No time, nothing newsworthy* → Funding, a contract, an Inc. 5000 spot, a clinical result, a new hire, your take on the caregiver shortage — all stories. We do research, pitching, drafting; you give 30 min/month.

**Where to source** — Rock Health / CB Insights / Crunchbase digital-health filters + Fierce/MedCity funding roundups; trade "who's news" columns (McKnight's, Home Health Care News, Becker's "Great Leaders"); awards (Inc. 5000 healthcare, business-journal fastest-growing/40-under-40); events (HLTH, ViVE, HIMSS; Home Care 100, NAHC, APTA CSM); registries (CMS Care Compare, Joint Commission/CHAP/ACHC, FDA 510(k) database); local angle for services (business-journal people-on-the-move, chamber awards, Google Maps clinic clusters by metro); cross-reference converted look-alikes (Praxis EMR, Body Helix, Royal Oaks, Rehabwheel, Medical Insurance Advocacy).

**Apollo / Sales Nav filters**
- Industries: Hospital & Health Care; Medical Devices; Health, Wellness & Fitness; Mental Health Care; IT & Services (health-keyword filtered).
- Titles: Founder, Co-Founder, CEO, Owner, President, Managing Partner, Executive Director. *Exclude: Director-of-Nursing-only, office manager, solo individual practitioner.*
- Headcount: **11–50 / 51–200** core; **2–10 ONLY if a funded startup or multi-location services business** (apply solo-operator gate).
- Keywords: "EMR" OR "EHR" OR "home health" OR "home care" OR "hospice" OR "physical therapy" OR "rehab" OR "digital health" OR "telehealth" OR "patient advocacy" OR "medical billing" OR "RPM" OR "care management" OR "medical device" OR "value-based care."
- Signals: raised funding last 6 mo (Seed/A/B); recent CMO/CNO/VP-Clinical hire; headcount growth >10%; clinician/caregiver job postings.
- Geo: US-wide for tech; **metro-by-metro for services** (buyers + recruits are local — prioritize Sun Belt / high-growth metros).
- **Exclude:** company name == person's name (solo operator), "Self-Employed"/"private practice"/"1:1 coaching" headlines, pure hospital/health-system *employees* (they don't sign vendor retainers).

### 1C. Government Contractors / Professional Services (GovCon)
*Federal/defense/IT/cyber firms, 8(a) small-biz primes & subs through PE-backed mid-tier. Buyer: founder/CEO/owner/President.*

**Why they buy PR**
- **Past-performance / proposal credibility** — COs and source-selection boards evaluate "corporate reputation." Earned media becomes citable proof in proposals, capability briefs, and on the site COs check before an award.
- **Teaming / prime relationships** — visible coverage of wins, certifications, and differentiated tech makes a BD lead at Leidos/Booz/SAIC return the call. PR is the warm intro at scale.
- **M&A / exit positioning** — 2026 is a hot GovCon M&A year; a documented coverage trail makes a founder a known, de-risked target, not a black box.
- **Recruiting cleared/technical talent** — the bottleneck. A visible CEO makes the firm a place top cleared candidates choose.
- **Category creation** in hot mission areas (AI-for-gov, autonomy, zero-trust/cyber, space) and **owner legacy** (Wash100 / WashingtonExec recognition).

**Trigger events** — new contract / task-order / recompete win (*the* announce-able moment); landing a big IDIQ vehicle (OASIS+/MAS, GWAC, agency BPA) or a prime's bid team; certifications (8(a) accept/graduate, CMMC L2, FedRAMP, FCL upgrade); funding/recap/PE investment or acquisition (buyer or seller); crossing a size/revenue threshold; senior hire from a prime or government (ex-SES, general/admiral); standing up a new capability in a hot mission area; awards (Wash100, WashingtonExec Pinnacle, GovCon Awards, SBA 8(a) Graduate of the Year, Inc. 5000); budget/policy tailwinds they can react to as an expert; milestone anniversaries.

**Decision-maker** — Founder/CEO/President signs and is the spokesperson — **a personal-credibility purchase as much as a company one.** Loops in: **(1) head of BD/Capture/Growth** (PR must serve pipeline and proposals or it won't get funded); (2) fractional/in-house Marketing/Comms if any; (3) in PE-backed firms, the **board/sponsor operating partner** wanting a clean pre-exit narrative. *Sell the CEO on legacy + pipeline; arm them to defend the spend to BD by tying coverage to past-performance and teaming.*

**Messaging angle** — *"Your contract wins, certifications, and mission expertise are buried in a SAM.gov notice nobody reads. We turn them into earned coverage in the outlets contracting officers, primes, and acquirers actually check — so your reputation does work in every proposal, teaming call, and exit conversation."*

**Proof points** — named coverage of a win/new capability in Washington Technology, GovCon Wire, or Federal News Network; CEO op-ed/expert commentary on a live mission/policy topic; exec named to Wash100 / WashingtonExec / a Chief Officer Award; a coverage trail a PE buyer/prime BD lead finds on page one of Google; recruiting/retention proof; before/after share-of-voice vs. named competitors.

**Target outlets** — **Washington Technology** (wins, Top 100, strategy), **GovCon Wire** (daily awards, M&A, exec moves — fast turnaround), **Federal News Network** (Contracts/Awards + DC radio), **Executive Mosaic network** (ExecutiveBiz, ExecutiveGov, Wash100/GovCon Awards), **Defense News**, **Nextgov/FCW & FedScoop** (gov IT/AI/cyber), **WashingtonExec** (exec Q&As, NoVA profiles), Washington Business Journal + the wire for the official announcement. *Pursue the beat, not just the outlet:* contracts/awards reporter, M&A reporter, cyber/AI mission reporter, exec-moves desk.

**Objections → reframes**
- *Pay-to-play / sponsored posts?* → Earned media. We pitch a real story (your win, capability, expertise) and reporters run it because it's news — which is exactly why it carries weight with COs. A sponsored post is invisible to the buying side.
- *Our work is sensitive/classified — nothing to talk about* → More is announce-able than you think: award value, agency, mission area, certs, hires, capability — covered daily. We work inside your OPSEC and PR-review constraints and lead with what you can disclose.
- *PR doesn't win contracts — it's price and past performance* → Exactly — earned coverage becomes citable proof of corporate reputation that strengthens the proposal and gets a prime's BD lead to take the teaming call so you're on the bid at all. We tie every placement to pipeline.
- *$8–15K/mo is a lot for a small/mid firm* → One incremental task order or teaming slot dwarfs the annual spend, and in a hot M&A year a coverage trail materially affects how a PE buyer values you. Positioning the asset, not an expense.
- *Tried PR, got one release that went nowhere* → A wire release is a notice, not earned media. We pitch named beat reporters a story they want and build sustained presence over a quarter — that's why it shows up when someone Googles you.

**Where to source** — GovCon Wire / Washington Technology award + exec-moves feeds (every named winner/hire is a fresh trigger); SBA dynamic search + 8(a) directory + recent acceptances/graduations; USASpending/SAM.gov/GovTribe/HigherGov (filter by award date + dollar threshold); award honorees & finalists (Wash100, WashingtonExec, GovCon Awards, SBA SB/8(a) of the Year); PE/M&A signals (Capstone, ACG DealSource A&D/GovCon, BusinessWire defense newsroom); associations & events (PSC, NDIA, AFCEA, ACT-IAC, NVTC); Inc. 5000 + WBJ fastest-growing filtered to gov/defense; LinkedIn win/cert/hire announcements.

**Apollo / Sales Nav filters**
- Industries: Defense & Space, Government Administration, IT & Services, Management Consulting, Security & Investigations, Computer & Network Security, Aviation & Aerospace, gov-focused Staffing.
- Titles: Founder, Co-Founder, CEO, President, Owner, Managing Partner, Principal · secondary: VP/SVP Business Development, Chief Growth Officer, VP Capture, Head of Marketing/Comms.

### 1D. Funded Tech / SaaS Founders Building Narrative
*Seed through Series B, VC-backed, building category + personal/company brand.*

**Why they buy PR**
- **Fundraising leverage** — tier-one coverage (TechCrunch, The Information, Forbes) is external validation a VC forwards internally and that supports the round narrative. Founders time PR to the raise.
- **Category creation** — in crowded AI/SaaS, the company that owns the framing owns the buyer's mental model. Be THE named voice on the trend before a competitor claims it.
- **Recruiting senior talent** — steady press signals momentum; top engineers/execs join companies that "matter and are visible."
- **Inbound pipeline + enterprise trust** — earned media lowers CAC and helps a sub-100-person vendor win procurement trust.
- **Founder-as-asset / personal brand** — incl. **AI-search visibility** (being the cited source in ChatGPT/Perplexity answers — the new 2026 credibility signal).
- **Competitive defense** — when a rival lands a big story, founders want air cover fast.

**Trigger events** — round closed or about to close (Seed/A/B — *the* #1 trigger); **raised-but-unannounced** (narrow window to place an exclusive before it leaks); product/major-feature launch (esp. AI/agentic) needing category framing; big-name customer/logo win; exec hire (VP Sales, CMO, CRO); growth milestone (ARR, users, "fastest-growing"); entering fundraising mode (pre-raise, 3–6 mo out — build a footprint to support valuation); competitor just raised/got a story (defensive); award/demo day/list inclusion (Forbes 30u30, Inc. 5000); negative press/outage/layoff (reputation defense, high-urgency).

**Decision-maker** — Founder/CEO is the economic buyer and signs directly — at seed–B there's rarely a CMO, so the founder owns it **personally and emotionally** (their face, their raise). Wants sign-off from: **(1) lead VC / board member** who often *pushes them toward* PR and may recommend agencies — *"your investors will love this"* accelerates the deal; (2) Head of Marketing/Growth at Series B+ (day-to-day contact, rarely the check-signer); (3) the business co-founder in a technical/business split. *Sell the founder; arm them with the investor framing.*

**Messaging angle** — *"You raised the round — now own the category before a competitor does. We get you and your company named in the outlets your next investors, recruits, and enterprise buyers already read (TechCrunch, The Information, Forbes), so the story works for you whether you're closing the next round, the next hire, or the next logo."* Always tie value back to the founder's near-term goal (raise / recruit / sell), never vanity.

**Proof points** — named tier-one placements (TechCrunch funding/launch, The Information feature, Forbes piece); a multi-outlet announcement package around the raise; founder thought-leadership footprint (bylines, podcasts, panels generating inbound); analyst/category recognition (Gartner/Forrester); **AI-search/GEO proof** (cited in ChatGPT/Perplexity/AI Overviews for the category); before/after on inbound demos, recruiter response, a VC saying "I saw you in X"; a stage-and-vertical-matched case study.

**Target outlets** — **TechCrunch** (funding/launch — AI, fintech, vertical SaaS), **The Information** (high-trust scoops — the credibility flex), **Forbes** (VC/startups + contributor + 30u30), Fortune Term Sheet, **Axios Pro Rata / Pro Enterprise Software** (Chris Metinko), Business Insider, **VentureBeat** (Michael Nunez — strongest AI/enterprise coverage), Fast Company/Inc., Crunchbase News, SiliconANGLE/The New Stack (dev-tool/infra), matched trade verticals (Finextra/American Banker for fintech, Modern Retail for commerce), podcasts/newsletters (Lenny's, SaaStr, Pro Rata).

**Objections → reframes**
- *Pay-to-play?* → Earned media, not advertising. We can't and don't pay journalists; they write on editorial judgment. That third-party validation is exactly why a VC or buyer trusts it — money can't manufacture it.
- *Heads-down / too early — PR after the next raise* → The press footprint is what *supports* the valuation in that raise. The week-of is too late; the best round coverage starts 60–90 days before. We build the runway now.
- *Can't I DIY / HARO / a friend at TechCrunch?* → A warm intro gets one story; it doesn't get you a category. The work is the angle, timing, and follow-through across a dozen outlets — a function, not a favor.
- *No measurable ROI* → We report against goals you measure — investor reply rates during the raise, recruiter/candidate response, inbound demos, and whether you show up when a buyer asks ChatGPT/Perplexity about your category.
- *$8–15K/mo is a lot at our stage* → A fractional comms hire costs more and can't open the same doors; one well-placed funding or category story shortens the raise, lowers CAC, and closes a senior hire. We scope to your runway.
- *Other agencies guarantee TechCrunch* → Anyone guaranteeing an outlet is selling paid placement or lying — no agency controls a newsroom. We commit to the target list, the relationships, and relentless execution.

**Where to source** — Crunchbase (recently-funded, "biggest rounds"); funding-tracker newsletters with founder contacts (Fundraise Insider, Growth List); TechCrunch funding/launch articles + "$100M+ raised" lists; Axios Pro Rata "First Look" (earliest signal, often pre-announcement); Product Hunt launches + Startup Battlefield cohorts; PitchBook (if budget); VC portfolio pages + demo-day cohorts (YC, Techstars, a16z/Sequoia/Benchmark); LinkedIn "we raised" posts + Sales Nav funding-event searches; SaaStr/Disrupt rosters.

- Titles: Founder, Co-Founder, CEO · secondary (Series B+): Head of Marketing, VP Marketing, Head of Growth, CMO. Management level: Founder/Owner, C-Suite.

## 4. How to Read a Reply — One-Page Scoring Guide

Score the **reply**, not the résumé. Sort every responder into one of three buckets in under 10 seconds.

### 🔥 HOT — book / advance now
The motion is *toward* a transaction. Examples in **bold** are from our data.

- **Cost question with curiosity attached** (the single best signal): *"Is there a cost to me in this? What types of folks are you working with…"* (**Stephanie NP-BC**), *"what's your business model… just curious"* (**Bryan Wish**).
- **Asks about logistics / scheduling:** proposes times, asks "what does your calendar look like," gives an email for the invite. **Robert Manasier, Saverio Pulizzi, Reza Moradi, Joy MPH, Melody Trimble, Trisha CPXP.**
- **Routes you to their team / VP** (delegating = real intent, not deflection): *"Pls work with my VP of marketing"* (**Divan Dave', OmniMD**), *"passing this over to McKenna"* (**Joel Idelson**).
- **"I'd be interested in hearing more / open to learn":** **Carrie Allen, Crystal Butler, David ICADC.**
- **Already books a slot / confirms a meeting:** **Tarshann Washington, Ishmael Osekre, Christopher Ph.D., Andrew MacDowell.**
> **Action:** respond within the hour, push to a discovery call, do NOT quote tier names/prices in the reply (Call-1 price rule).

### 🟡 WARM — qualify before you invest
Interest is real but a blocker is showing. One sharp question decides it.

- **"Maybe" / "reach back out later" / "not right now but will keep in mind":** **Karim Hageali "Maybe,"** **Chris Cloud** (call in a couple weeks), **Mike Harms, Vida Clark.** → Pin a specific date; if they won't commit a date, downgrade.
- **Soft objection that's reframable:** "no time / nothing newsworthy," "I have a marketing person." → Run the matching reframe from §1. If it's a *real* existing PR partner (**Jerry Vinci, Laura Miller, Mark Westlake, Jay Schweid**), it's a NO — log and move on.
- **Cost question that might be a gate:** if the *next* sentence reveals free-or-nothing, drop to DEAD. If they engage on value, promote to HOT.
> **Action:** send exactly one qualifying question. Convert or kill — don't nurture indefinitely.

### ⚫ DEAD — log reason, stop
Reject on a structural mechanism. Don't re-pitch.

- **"Is this free?" as a gate / free-or-nothing:** **Dr. Isler** *"Is this free?"*, **Payal M.D.** *"only if… free of cost,"* **Amy Schrier** *"no budget if there is a fee."*
- **"Pay-to-play, fees out of line":** **Perry Grosser**; **Ziad Abdelnour** *"not paying anything to be covered."*
- **Can't / won't spend:** **Josh Sandler** *"don't think I'd want to invest money,"* **Farzad Khosravi** *"sadly I cannot afford this."*
- **Hard no / opt-out / STOP:** "pass," "Opt out," "STOP," "no thanks," "not interested," **Waseem** *"I have a big following on OnlyFans… I'm good."*
- **Already has PR / all set:** **Jerry Vinci, Laura Miller, Mark Westlake, Jay Schweid, Bill Praschak.**
- **Anti-ICP segment** (§2) regardless of tone — incl. the warm-but-broke ministry/nonprofit/education replies.
- **OOO / left company / wrong person / bounce / verification wall:** auto-junk (most of the EM block — Alan Silbert, Marty Kurtz, Lee Woolley, Ed Bley "wrong guy," the legacyinsuranceagency.com spam-verify loop).
> **Action:** mark dead with reason code, suppress from re-sends.

### The 3 questions to ask of any reply
1. **Which direction is the motion?** Toward logistics/price/team = HOT. Toward "later/maybe" = WARM. Toward "no/free/already-have" = DEAD.
2. **Is the cost question curiosity or a gate?** Curiosity (asks what they'd get) = HOT. Gate (free-or-nothing) = DEAD. *This is the highest-value judgment you make.*
3. **Is the block structural or situational?** Structural (regulated speech, no budget, mission-funded, has-PR) = DEAD. Situational (timing, no-news, busy) = WARM, run the reframe.

**Remember:** title and vertical got them into the inbox. The *reply* tells you whether to spend the next hour on them. Score the behavior.
